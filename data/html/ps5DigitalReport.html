PS5 Digital: Cost

The disc-less PS5 Digital Edition is the cheaper option at £359 / $399 / AU$599.

PS5 Digital: Design and Build

The PS5 Digital is the smaller, more lightweight model of the two, with the overall dimensions coming in 12mm slimmer the regular model and overall weight coming in half a kilo lighter (500g). Both consoles have a sculpted, sci-fi look to them and can be vertically rather than horizontally. The PS5 is not completely inaudible in a silent room, but the consistent whir is quiet enough to be drowned out by any sound coming from your TV or sound system. The user interface, which includes a new home screen with game ‘cards’, is fresh, super-stylish, logical and snappy. It's also familiar enough to ensure that existing PS4 gamers can quickly find their way about.

PS5 Digital: Specs

Spec-wise, both PS5 consoles feature the same AMD Zen 2-based CPU, the same 16GB GDDR6 / 256-bit memory, and the same 825GB SSD. The way Sony has designed and integrated the PS5’s storage makes it so fast (more than twice as fast as that of the Series X) that it boosts overall console performance. Neither PS5 has an 8K output option. Instead, silky- smooth 4K at 60Hz is the performance target, with 120Hz available via some games, sometimes at the cost of resolution and/or certain graphical features. Unfortunately, unlike the regular model, without a disc-drive, you're unable to transfer games and game data from your old PS4, with this being one of the main limitations with the cheaper digitalised console variant.

PS5 Digital: Entertainment Features

Eyeing up the PS5 as an entertainment hub as well as a games machine? The PS5 and PS5 Digital Edition are a great choice. They now support Netflix, Disney Plus, Apple TV, Spotify, Twitch, YouTube, Amazon Prime Video, Hulu, Peacock, Disney Plus, HBO Max and PlayStation Video. There's also a rumour that Apple Music is headed to the PS5 at some point in the not-so-distant future.

PS5 Digital: Audio

On a more positive note, both PS5 and PS5 Digital offer Sony's proprietary 'gold standard' 3D audio technology. The PS5's 3D Audio engine, 'Tempest', produces open, spacious and atmospheric sound with good placement of effects. And although Dolby Atmos isn't an option for games, it is for the PS5 disc edition, which can do a very good job of Dolby Atmos soundtracks when given the chance. It doesn’t quite have the crispness of a dedicated game, but it does produce a room-filling sound with good clarity. Unfortunately, the PS5 digital lacks the feature of Dolby Atmos, which is only available on the more expensive model PlayStation 5 that comes with the disc-drive.
